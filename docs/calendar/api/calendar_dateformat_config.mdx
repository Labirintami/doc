<Descr>
 
dhtmlxCalendar uses the following characters for setting a date format:

| Character		|	Definition
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------|
|<strong>%d</strong>			|day as a number with leading zero, 01..31		|
|<strong>%j</strong>			|day as a number, 1..31							|
|<strong>%D</strong>			|short name of the day, Su Mo Tu...				|
|<strong>%l</strong>			|full name of the day, Sunday Monday Tuesday...	|
|<strong>%m</strong>			|month as a number with leading zero, 01..12	|
|<strong>%n</strong>			|month as a number, 1..12						|
|<strong>%M</strong>			|short name of the month, Jan Feb Mar...		|
|<strong>%F</strong>			|full name of the month, January February March...|
|<strong>%y</strong>			|year as a number, 2 digits						|
|<strong>%Y</strong>			|year as a number, 4 digits						|
|<strong>%h</strong>			|hours 12-format with leading zero, 01..12)		|
|<strong>%g</strong>			|hours 12-format, 1..12)						|
|<strong>%H</strong>			|hours 24-format with leading zero, 00..23		|
|<strong>%G</strong>			|hours 24-format, 0..23							|
|<strong>%i</strong>			|minutes with leading zero, 01..59				|
|<strong>%s</strong>			|seconds with leading zero, 01..59				|
|<strong>%a</strong>			|am or pm										|
|<strong>%A</strong>			|AM or PM										|
|<strong>%u</strong>			|milliseconds									|




</Descr>
